Temperature and Humidity Information

Why Temperature and Humidity Matter

Temperature and humidity play a crucial role in determining the comfort and growth of plants, animals, and even people. Understanding how temperature and humidity levels affect various ecosystems is essential for maintaining a balanced environment.

Temperature and Humidity Guidelines

Ideal Temperature

The ideal temperature for most plants ranges between 65°F and 75°F (18°C to 24°C). Temperatures outside of this range may stress the plants, affecting their growth.

Humidity Levels

Humidity levels between 40% and 60% are considered ideal for most indoor plants. Too much humidity can cause mold, while too little can result in dry leaves and poor plant growth.

High Temperature

Temperatures exceeding 85°F (29°C) can be damaging to many plants. High temperatures may lead to wilting, leaf burn, and poor plant performance.

Low Temperature

Temperatures below 50°F (10°C) may lead to frost damage in plants. Cold-sensitive plants should be kept away from drafts and frost-prone areas.
